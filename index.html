My Projects

HDAnalyzeR

HDAnalyzeR is an R package I developed while working for the Human Disease Blood Atlas group, designed to facilitate proteomics analysis for biomarker selection from blood plasma samples. It is optimized to work with Olink proteomics data, but it can be adapted to other proteomics platforms. HDAnalyzeR offers ready-to-use functions for common proteomics tasks such as protein differential expression analysis, classification models, imputation methods, dimensionality reduction, and data visualization, aiming to streamline workflows and enhance the standardization and efficiency of biomarker discovery in groups research.

Protein Secondary Structure Prediction

In this bioinformatics project within the Molecular Techniques in Life Science master’s program, I aimed to enhance protein secondary structure prediction using neural networks. By utilizing multiple sequence alignment data and a sliding window approach, I improved prediction accuracy. Techniques like dropout and ensemble modeling further optimized model performance. Evaluation metrics demonstrated the model’s effectiveness in distinguishing between secondary structure classes. Our results underscored the importance of hyperparameter optimization and architectural design in boosting prediction accuracy, showcasing the potential of neural networks for precise protein structure analysis in bioinformatics.

Rheological Analysis Toolbox

The Rheological Analysis Toolbox was a part of my undergraduate thesis project and is a powerful set of tools designed for rheological analysis and data processing of synovial fluid samples, with a primary focus on facilitating Osteoarthritis (OA) diagnosis. This toolbox offers functionalities such as database creation, sample analysis, technical report generation, statistical analysis, biomarker validation, and machine learning model training. It streamlines the entire process from data collection to interpretation, empowering researchers to make data-driven decisions and advance medical knowledge in OA diagnosis.
